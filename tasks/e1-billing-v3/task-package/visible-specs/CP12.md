# CP12: Dunning on payment failure with entitlement gating

payment_failed events move an active subscription to past_due and count dunning attempts per subscription; a capture that pays the open invoice in full recovers the subscription to active and resets the counter. The entitlement query gates product access: full while trialing or active; grace while past_due through the second failed attempt; none from the third failed attempt onward or once canceled. Registry delta: append the field `dunning` to the subscription registry list (after `scheduled_change`); it renders as a nested record with its own registry [`attempts`, `entered_at`] and is omitted while null.

## Invariants introduced (cumulative from now on)

- I-STATE extends: active -> past_due on failure; past_due -> active on full recovery.
- I-ENTITLE: entitlement derives only from lifecycle status and the dunning attempt count.

## Scenarios

#### Scenario: Failure enters dunning
- **GIVEN** an active subscription and a payment_failed event
- **WHEN** the subscription view is queried
- **THEN** the status is past_due with attempt count 1

#### Scenario: Full capture recovers
- **GIVEN** a past_due subscription whose open invoice is captured in full
- **WHEN** the subscription view is queried
- **THEN** the status is active and the attempt count is 0

#### Scenario: Grace during early dunning
- **GIVEN** a subscription with one or two failed attempts
- **WHEN** entitlement is queried
- **THEN** the entitlement is grace

#### Scenario: Cut off at the third failure
- **GIVEN** a subscription with three failed attempts
- **WHEN** entitlement is queried
- **THEN** the entitlement is none

## Events introduced

- `payment_failed` with fields: `at`, `event_id`, `invoice_id`, `type`

## Queries introduced

- `entitlement` with fields: `kind`, `subscription_id`
