# QA / Testing Review — E5 Stage-1 Prereg v2

## A. VERDICT

**Fix first.** The skeleton holds and most of v2's hardening is real, but the plan currently freezes with (a) the event-counting semantics of its own likely-primary undefined, (b) an internal contradiction between the weaker-reading rule and the stated MDE, and (c) a vacuity hole in the uncovered-slice claim rule that the coverage-map method actively feeds — none survivable past seal.

## B. FINDINGS (ranked by severity)

**F1. §5 × §11.4 — Event multiplicity is undefined for the event-count primary-in-force (R3 fix incomplete).**
Attack: §5 defines a regression event as "passed at some j, fails at k > j." Under the binary that's harmless; under the ceiling-branch Wilcoxon it's fatal ambiguity: does a check that passes at 2 and fails at 3, 4, 5 contribute one event or three? Does a flapping check (pass-fail-pass-fail) re-arm? A `chain_broken` at checkpoint 3 of an 8-chain, under the literal reading, fails every previously-passed check at every subsequent checkpoint — event counts scale with remaining chain length, and the Wilcoxon is then dominated by one broken checkpoint per chain. Failure scenario: the branch triggers (see F5 — plausibly ~1/3 of worlds), the analyst chooses a counting convention after seeing data, and the primary is an unfrozen choice. Fix: pin one convention before freeze (recommend adjacent-transition: event at k iff the check passed on the final graded snapshot at k−1 and fails at k), and pin `chain_broken` cascade handling in counts (e.g., broken checkpoints contribute at most one composite event per check, with the ★ chain-broken sensitivity unchanged).

**F2. §6.2/§13.3 vs §11.2 — The weaker-reading rule's "p crosses α" definition contradicts the stated MDE (R7 fix incomplete; genuine internal contradiction).**
Attack: the provenance sensitivity re-runs the primary on 23 native pairs. Under a *true* ~35 pp effect, the 23-pair subset crosses α frequently for pure power reasons — no inconsistency, just fewer pairs. As written, "p crosses α" = material disagreement = claim takes the weaker reading. Failure scenario: full-sample p = 0.03, native-only p = 0.08, same sign and magnitude — a legitimate win is mechanically demoted to qualified language. The effective MDE of an *unqualified* claim is therefore the native-subset MDE (~40 pp), while §11.2 advertises ~35 pp. Fix: for strict-subset sensitivities, define disagreement by estimate stability (sign flip, or point estimate shrinking below some pre-set fraction, e.g. half), reserving α-crossing for same-N sensitivities (flake rule, tamper exclusion); or keep the rule and amend §11.2 to state that the unqualified claim is powered at ~40 pp. Either is honest; the current pair of texts is not mutually consistent.

**F3. §3.2 — The mapping method's failure mode is directional: mechanical name-matching under-labels coverage, contaminating the uncovered slice with mirrored checks (R1+r22 stack breaks).**
Attack: a first-pass matcher on names/assertions systematically misses semantic coverage (a hidden check asserting behavior a scenario covers under different vocabulary). Every such miss puts a *covered* check into the *uncovered* bin — and covered checks move with treatment by construction. Failure scenario: Δ_uncovered mirrors Δ_covered because the "uncovered" upstream checks are substantially mislabeled mirrors; criterion 1b passes; the unscoped Level-4 claim publishes on a slice that was never independent. The r22 audit (uniform ≥10%/≥15 checks, >10% disagreement threshold) does not contain this: a 9% mislabel rate passes the audit while being concentrated exactly in the decision-critical class. Fix: concentrate audit budget where the decision lives — human-audit **100% of upstream checks labeled uncovered** (held-out probes are uncovered by construction and need no audit; the upstream-uncovered class is small and is the entire evidential basis of 1b). Publish per-check labels.

**F4. §6.2 criterion 1b — The ≥1-event floor is precisely the vacuity R11 was written to kill.**
Attack: one uncovered regression event in C, zero in T, gives Δ_uncovered < 0 with "≥1 event observed" — the unscoped claim's independence requirement is satisfied by a single stochastic event. The plan applied a ≥2-discordance minimum-evidence rule to the codex criterion for exactly this reason and then left its more important sibling at floor 1. Failure scenario: hostile methodologist's opening line writes itself: "the generalization claim rests on n = 1." Fix: R11-grade evidence floor — e.g., ≥3 uncovered events across arms contributed by ≥2 distinct chains; below it, auto-scope to "literal promises on the checked surface" exactly as the rule already does for the adverse/empty cases.

**F5. §11.4 — The ceiling-branch trigger has no stated operating characteristics; by my arithmetic it fires ~1/3 of the time under the plan's own central prior [VERIFY].**
Attack: R2a forced an error table onto the floor gate; the ceiling gate (p̂_C ≥ 0.75, i.e., ≥6/8 pilot chains) got none. Exact binomial on n = 8: at true p_C = 0.60 (the modal power-table row), P(≥6/8) ≈ 0.32; at true 0.85, P(miss) ≈ 0.11 [VERIFY — my computation]. Failure scenario: in roughly one-in-three worlds where the binary primary would have been well-behaved, the plan switches to the event-count primary — the one whose semantics F1 shows are undefined — and a critic can say the "pre-registered primary" was closer to a coin-flip-selected pair. Fix: publish the ceiling error table in §9-style; consider a two-key corroboration (chain rate AND transition-level rate above a stated ceiling) for symmetry with the floor; at minimum, accept the ~1/3 figure explicitly on record.

**F6. §2.2/§2.3/§5 — The frozen-catalog reference is unspecified against lifecycle supersessions: what exactly does the runner execute at checkpoint k?**
Attack: the T-suite hash is defined against "frozen catalog text," but the archive step legitimately rewrites MODIFIED scenarios and lifecycle metadata legitimately retires checks. If the frozen catalog holds only per-checkpoint delta specs, then either every genuine supersession trips permanent "drift" logging (noise that trains the operator to ignore the tamper channel), or T's runner executes retired scenarios — the agent is executable-instructed to preserve behavior the current spec superseded, colliding with new requirements (guard-metric damage against T; a null manufactured by the machinery). Fix: the frozen catalog must contain per-checkpoint **accumulated-active** scenario sets, and rung 2 gets a three-way assertion: runner's executed set == frozen accumulated-active set == lifecycle metadata's active set, at every checkpoint.

**F7. §5/§12.5 — No pair-symmetric exclusion discipline for infra exclusions and replay-invalid runs.**
Attack: checkpoint-level infra exclusion and run-level non-replay-validity are defined per arm. In a paired design, excluding one arm's checkpoint (or run) while keeping the other silently unbalances exposure within the pair — and artifact loss is not outcome-neutral (crashed runs correlate with broken states correlate with regressions), so one-sided exclusion is a selection channel in whichever direction luck runs. Failure scenario: three T runs lose trajectory files to a disk fault, are dropped as non-replay-valid, their C counterparts stay in — headline computed on asymmetric pairs. Fix: a unit (checkpoint or run) excluded for infra/replay reasons in either arm is excluded from the paired primary in **both** arms; per-arm exclusion counts published; a pre-set cap (e.g., >10% of pairs affected → integrity flag on the whole result).

**F8. §2.4/§15-item-6 — The parity validator is pre-flight; the divergence risks are mid-run.**
Attack: injection happens per checkpoint (`_setup_for_checkpoint`), and hazard (iii) — archive step × T's mirrored feature files — is a mid-run interaction by definition. A pre-flight byte-diff cannot see checkpoint-5 rendering, and a rendering path that truncates or reorders scenario text in one arm only (C's text comes from injection, T's mirrors from a separate deterministic extraction — two code paths) diverges the *effective spec* between arms invisibly. Fix: (a) the validator dry-renders **all** checkpoints of a chain pre-flight, not just entry; (b) a runtime assert at every checkpoint entry that C-workspace and T-workspace scenario text are byte-identical (modulo the declared T-delta); divergence = validity flag on the pair.

**F9. §10.1 — The R13 feedback-localization check is theater as specified.**
Attack: "a sample of induced failures" — induced by whom, how many, and what passes? Operator-crafted failures will be single-assertion, cleanly-diffed, unrepresentatively localizable; organic failures cascade across scenarios. Failure scenario: the check passes on five easy mutants, the confirmatory feedback channel is unactionable on real cross-scenario breakage, the null publishes *without* the "feedback throttled" scoping it should have carried — R13's whole purpose defeated. Fix: pre-register the generation rule (seeded mutants on golden solutions across ≥3 archetypes: wrong value, missing behavior, cross-scenario interaction) and a numeric bar (e.g., ≥4/5 localized from trajectory evidence, rubric stated); any archetype scoring 0 is recorded and binds the §1.4 null scoping.

**F10. §3.1/§3.3 — Probe authoring necessarily reads the scenarios; nothing constrains probe proximity, and the covered/uncovered *partition itself* is an unfrozen experimenter choice.**
Attack: the firewall blocks visible→hidden tuning but the probe author must read scenario text to author "behavior not asserted by any visible scenario" — near-covered probes (behaviorally adjacent to scenario clauses) make the uncovered slice covariate with the covered one, compounding F3. Separately: which spec-required behaviors get scenario-ized at all is the operator's free choice, sealed but never disciplined — the operator effectively chooses what the treatment gets direct signal on. Fix: a pre-registered scenario-selection rule (e.g., all top-level acceptance criteria are scenario-ized; probes drawn only from spec-required behaviors deliberately excluded, with the exclusion decisions listed) plus a published per-probe nearest-scenario note. Anything less, disclose in §16 as an O1-class residual with direction unassessed.

**F11. §10.1 — Referee/golden invisibility from inside the agent container is asserted, never tested.**
Attack: "upstream native; verdict item 10" is a codebase-reading claim, and the fork adds mounts (OpenSpec workspace, mirrored files, runner). One mount mistake exposes hidden tests or golden solutions to both arms — contamination that biases null-ward (both arms copy) or arbitrarily (partial copying). Fix: rung-2 assertion — from inside each arm's running container, scan the filesystem for referee-file markers, golden-solution content, and the frozen catalog path; must come back empty; result in the manifest.

**F12. §13.5 — Immutability scopes metric *definitions* but not metric *implementation* (R12 fix incomplete).**
Attack: §13.5(c) freezes "hidden referee, visible assets, checkpoint lists, and metrics" after the first rung-3 outcome — but the event-computation and claim-rule *code* is not on that list and its hash is not explicitly in the §12.5 seal. A post-outcome "bug fix" to eligibility bookkeeping is the classic forking path, and F1's ambiguity is exactly where it would happen. Fix: analysis-implementation hash enters the freeze manifest; any post-outcome change to it produces a logged changelist and dual reporting (old and new results) under the weaker-reading rule.

**F13. §9 — R2's own standard applied to half of R2: the transition key and the joint gate have no operating characteristics.**
Attack: the R2a table quantifies the chain key only. The transition key's denominator (~35–40 realized eligible transitions) is itself random, its ≥4 threshold has unstated error rates, and the joint two-key gate's behavior depends on chain–transition correlation nobody has modeled. Failure scenario: the operator ratifies "the recommended default" believing its properties are the R2a table's, when the binding gate is the untabulated conjunction. Fix: state joint-gate pass probabilities under 2–3 plausible correlation assumptions, or the operator explicitly ratifies the transition key as unquantified (R2c-style, on record).

**F14. §6.2 criterion 3 — The 10 pp new-pass guard is unanchored and permits treatment to buy the primary with conservative minimalism.**
Attack: the runner tells T's agent exactly which existing promises are at risk; the dominant safe strategy is minimal new implementation that keeps the suite green. At a plausible C new-pass rate of ~50%, T at 40.1% passes the guard while shipping ~20% less functionality in relative terms — and the headline is a regression win. Fix: anchor the guard at freeze-of-N to the pilot-measured C new-pass rate (control-conditioned, so §13.5(a)-legal if declared now): e.g., guard = min(10 pp absolute, 15% relative). Declining this is acceptable but must be an on-record accepted risk, because it is the single most quotable hostile attack on a positive result.

**F15. §6.3/§11.2 — The exposure-normalized hazard conditions on a post-treatment variable and the plan overclaims what it "separates."**
Attack: which checks become eligible is an outcome of arm behavior; T's eligible set is plausibly compositionally different — the iterate-to-green loop produces more boundary-marginal implementations that clear the 3/3 eligibility bar and then break under fail-any (a mechanism hypothesis, flagged as such). The per-transition hazard then compares different populations of promises, and "separates 'more promises kept' from 'same per-promise breakage'" is stronger language than a post-treatment-conditioned ratio supports. Fix: reword the secondary as descriptive with a pre-registered caveat that both the binary (conservative against T) and the normalized hazard (post-treatment-conditioned, bias direction unknown) are imperfect in different directions; interpretation triangulates, neither adjudicates.

**F16. §14 input 3 — The codex-slice draw procedure is undefined.**
Attack: "drawn from the confirmatory pool" without a rule is an experimenter degree of freedom; even pre-outcome, chain characteristics are visible and selection can tilt evaluability or direction. Fix: seeded random draw, stratified by chain length, seed derived from the freeze commit — the §12.1 discipline already exists; apply it here.

**F17. §1.4/§4 — "Executable acceptance feedback" names one mechanism; the T-delta is a three-component bundle, and the required C-curve has no wording consequence.**
Attack: if the decomposition shows C1-vs-T1 (deterrence — mere presence of executable checks) carries most of Δ, the headline's causal noun is mechanistically wrong even though the contrast is valid; separately, the mandatory C-attempt-1-vs-C-final curve can reveal the ritual *harmed* control (TDAD direction) — the claim survives as worded but a critic reads it as "beat a degraded control," and the plan currently only requires publishing the curve, not reacting to it. Fix (both under-claiming, so beneath the binding maximum): pre-commit (a) if the attempt-1 gap is ≥ ~2/3 of the final gap, headline wording shifts to "supplied executable acceptance checks"; (b) if C-final shows materially more regressions than C-attempt-1, the claim carries an explicit ritual-harm flag — symmetrically noted if the ritual helped.

**F18. §12.7 — Rate-limit asymmetry is logged into a review with no pre-stated consequence.**
Attack: paired back-to-back runs share subscription state; quota exhaustion or heavy backoff hits whichever arm runs second — symmetric in expectation, materially asymmetric in realization at N = 33. If backoff retries consume the step budget, the throttled arm gets fewer effective work steps: a stochastic parity violation. "Included in the validity-flag review" with no rule is post-hoc adjudication. Fix: pre-state a threshold (asymmetric HIT_RATE_LIMITED exposure within a pair above X) that routes the pair into a ★ sensitivity exclusion under the weaker-reading rule; rung 2 asserts whether backoff consumes step budget.

**F19. §7 — Flake screens run on golden solutions only; agent-code-dependent flakiness is unscreened, and the visible completion-time run has no repetition rule.**
Attack: goldens exercise one implementation's timing surface; agent implementations (buffering, subprocess choices) can manifest flakiness the screen never saw. Referee-side, 3× grading contains the damage; visible-side, a single-run false failure at completion sends T's revise turn chasing a ghost (null-ward), and a false pass wastes the guaranteed run. Fix: state the visible-run repetition policy explicitly (single-run accepted as disclosed noise is fine; or apply the class rule visibly for the enumerated interactive class); log visible-vs-referee verdict flips on covered checks in-run as a live extension of the R6 statistic.

**F20. §9/§11.4 — Floor-gate validity decays across the pilot→confirmatory gap: composition (30% authored, unmeasured) and calendar time.**
Attack: the gate is measured on 8 native chains and licenses spend on a pool that is 30% authored (O3 admits the base-rate hole, but the gate's *pass* is silently generalized to that pool); and weeks may elapse between rung 1 and rung 3, during which the provider can ship behavioral changes under an unchanged pinned model ID [VERIFY — external assumption, not verifiable from the plan]. Fix: gate language scoped to native chains explicitly; a freshness rule (rung-3 start more than X weeks after rung 1 → a 2-chain subscription-only control spot-check against the pilot rate before treatment spend).

## C. INSISTED GUARDRAILS

**G1 — Metric-integrity seal.** Before freeze: (a) event multiplicity and chain-broken cascade handling pinned in §5 for the event-count primary (F1); (b) pair-symmetric exclusion rule for infra/replay-invalid units with published per-arm counts and a cap (F7); (c) analysis-implementation hash in the freeze manifest with dual-reporting on any post-outcome change (F12). Without all three, the primary is not actually frozen — only its prose is.

**G2 — Uncovered-slice integrity package.** (a) Human audit of 100% of upstream checks labeled uncovered (F3); (b) criterion 1b evidence floor raised to R11 grade — ≥3 uncovered events across ≥2 chains, else auto-scope (F4). This is the load-bearing wall between "generalizes beyond the checked surface" and "kept its literal promises"; as written it is one mislabel plus one lucky event thick.

**G3 — Tested, not asserted, isolation and parity.** Two runtime items enter the rung-2 checklist and per-run telemetry: per-checkpoint cross-arm scenario-text hash equality including all-checkpoint dry-render pre-flight (F8), and an in-container referee/golden invisibility probe (F11). Each may be declined only with a recorded reason, because both convert the design's two most trusted assumptions from claims into measurements at near-zero cost.

## D. TOP 3 RISKS

1. The analysis most likely to actually run (event-count primary, ~1/3 trigger under the central prior [VERIFY]) is the least specified analysis in the document.
2. The strongest surviving win-fabrication path is a contaminated "uncovered" slice plus a one-event floor — the generalization claim can currently rest on n = 1 mislabeled evidence.
3. The weaker-reading-by-α mechanic makes the real unqualified-claim MDE ~40 pp, structurally biasing the program toward "mixed/qualified" mush rather than either clean outcome — the worst result for a skeptical-CTO audience.
